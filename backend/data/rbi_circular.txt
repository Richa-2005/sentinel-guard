--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 To maintain mathematical and ledger uniformity, fraud profiles are mapped directly to provisions of the Indian Penal Code:

   (c) Unauthorised credit extensions for reward or illegal gratification.
   (d) Negligence and cash shortages.
   (e) Cheating and forgery.
   (f) Irregularities in foreign exchange transactions.

2.2 Core Quantitative Shortcuts for Automated Systems:
   - Cash shortages exceeding 10,000 units must be treated as fraudulent transactions out-of-the-box.

   - In cases of forged instruments, reporting and police tracking duties belong strictly to the paying banker (the entity that releases the payment or suffers the asset loss).

3.1 VELOCITY PROFILE TIERING (LESS THAN 1.00 LAKH)
Individual compliance incidents involving amounts under 1.00 Lakh are handled via aggregate analytical telemetry streams. They do not require custom standalone filings but must be compiled into standard structural Quarterly Statements (Form FMR-2) within 15 days of the closing quarter.

3.2 SUSPICIOUS VOLUMETRIC INCIDENTS (1.00 LAKH TO 25.00 LAKH)
Any anomalous fraud vector scaling to 1.00 Lakh or higher must be compiled individual case-by-case inside the standardized FMR-1 matrix format and filed securely with the Regional Office of Urban Banks Department within three (3) weeks of target identification.

3.3 CRITICAL COMPLIANCE OUTLIERS (25.00 LAKH AND ABOVE)
Transactions crossing the 25.00 Lakh cap represent severe operational system failures. These events require direct D.O. notification to the Principal Chief General Manager of the Department of Banking Supervision within seven (7) days of realization, alongside an un-clubbed FMR-1 submission within 3 weeks.
